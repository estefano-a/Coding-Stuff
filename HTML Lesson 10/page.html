Robotics for Beginners: Building Your First Bot


Dive into the world of robotics and discover the magic of creating machines that can move, sense, and interact with the environment. This beginner-friendly course is designed to introduce participants to the foundational concepts of robotics, from understanding the basic components to programming their very first robot.     2. The history and evolution of robots, various types of robots and their applications and the impact of robotics on society and industries.

  2. Robot Anatomy: Understanding Components

    3. Motors, sensors, and actuators: Their roles and functions, power sources for robots and selecting the right components for specific tasks.

    6. Wheels, legs, and propellers: Different modes of robot movement, basics of robot kinematics and designing for stability and maneuverability

  6. Robot Communication

    7. Basics of wireless communication: Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, RF as well as remote control vs. autonomous decision-making and ensuring secure communication.

  7. Designing and Assembling Your Robot

    8. Sketching and planning your robot design, hands-on assembly workshop and testing and troubleshooting common issues.

  8. Future of Robotics: Trends and Career Opportunities

    9. Advanced robotics and AI integration, ethical considerations in robotics and potential career paths and further learning resources.
