Course Progress

Modules:

  1. Introduction to Robotics

    2. The history and evolution of robots, various types of robots and their applications and the impact of robotics on society and industries.

  2. Robot Anatomy: Understanding Components

    3. Motors, sensors, and actuators: Their roles and functions, power sources for robots and selecting the right components for specific tasks.

  3. The Brain of the a Robot: Introduction to programming

    4. Basic programming concepts, using platforms like Arduino and Raspberry Pi as well as writing your first robot script.

  4. Sensors and Feedback

    5. Different types of sensors: infrared, ultrasonic, touch, and more as well as how robots perceive their environment and programming robots to react to stimuli.

  5. Locomotion: Making Your Robot Move

    6. Wheels, legs, and propellers: Different modes of robot movement, basics of robot kinematics and designing for stability and maneuverability

  6. Robot Communication

    7. Basics of wireless communication: Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, RF as well as remote control vs. autonomous decision-making and ensuring secure communication.

  7. Designing and Assembling Your Robot

    8. Sketching and planning your robot design, hands-on assembly workshop and testing and troubleshooting common issues.

  8. Future of Robotics: Trends and Career Opportunities

    9. Advanced robotics and AI integration, ethical considerations in robotics and potential career paths and further learning resources.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Q: What will I learn in the "Introduction to Robotics" module?

    A: In this module, you'll explore the history and evolution of robots, various types of robots and their applications, and the impact of robotics on society and industries.


    Q: What can I expect to gain from the "Future of Robotics: Trends and Career Opportunities" section?

    A: This module explores advanced robotics and AI integration, ethical considerations in robotics, potential career paths, and further learning resources to help you prepare for the future in this field.


    Q: Are there any prerequisites for enrolling in this course?

    A: There are no strict prerequisites, but having a basic understanding of programming concepts can be helpful.
