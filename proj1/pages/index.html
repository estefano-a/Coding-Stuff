How the Internet works

The World Wide Web and Browsers

This is a system of interconnected webpages on the internet. Users can access this webcontent through browsers such as Chrome, Firefox or Vivaldi.

DNS

DNS (Domain Name System) is basically the internet's phonebook, as it translates domain names into IP addresses.

IP Addresses

IP (Internet Protocol) addresses are numerical labels assigned to devices on a network, including the internet. They are unique to every single device.

Protocols

Protocols are rules and regulations that control communication between devices and systems on the internet. They ensure that data is transmitted, recieved and interpreted correctly.

Servers and Clients

Servers are robust computing systems designed to store and manage data and information from websites and users. Clients are the devices such as smartphones or laptops.

Latency and Bandwidth

Latency refers to the delay in data transmission over a network, measured in milliseconds. Bandwidth refers to the maximum amount of data that can be transmitted in a given time.

Cookies and Caching

Cookies store user data for website preferences and tracking, while caching speeds up webpage access by temporarily storing content.
